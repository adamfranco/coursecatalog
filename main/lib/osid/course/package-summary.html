Defines the interfaces necessary to describe the course * service.

* *

The Course OSID provides course management services for educational * offerings or other gatherings related to an event or curriculum. Courses * may be used to model a class, conference session, or even a meetup group. *

* *

The Course OSID defines two course interfaces. A CourseOffering * describes a scheduled course. Courses may be stored in a * "canonical" form for reusability among multiple CourseOfferings * using the Course interface. Both Courses * and CourseOfferings may be organized into * CourseCatalogs that may reflect a provider, program, term or some * other organizational scheme. A CourseOffering maps a * Course to a Term and location. A Term * is an arbitrary period of time in which CourseOfferings * occur. Terms may also be hierarchical to capture smaller * periods of time within larger periods of time. The location can be * represented as a Resource to normalize a fixed location * identity such as a room. The can also be described as a display string, or * both.

* *

A CourseOffering may also relate additional resources. A * Calendar can be used to convey multiple meeting times or * other milestones such as exams or due dates pertinent to the course. A * learning objective can be used to relate the CourseOffering * to a stated objective that in turn provides relationships to assessments * and other course ofefrings an materials. The CourseOffering * may also include a URL to identify an external course web site or learning * management system.

* *

[enrollment] In addition to the cataloging, Courses and * CourseOfferings may also be mapped to Topics. * A Topic provides a tagging mechanism to allow browsing or * searching by subject matter. Topics may also be hierarchical * and organized into CourseCatalogs.

* *

The Course OSID at its most basic level provides the means of defining * a course or program catalog. It contains many optional services and * relationships to execute various course management functions.
